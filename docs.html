Per-Benchmark Key Insights

Metacognition

RLHF Paradox confirmed: 0.92 resilience vs 0.38 deference. Monitoring (0.92) strong, Control (0.38) absent. FOK dropped from 1.00→0.68, IOED collapsed 0.62→0.13 under load.

Executive Functions

WCST reveals COLOR saliency bias — model defaults to color when multiple sorting rules are valid. Tower shows bimodal failure: under-planning easy problems, over-generalizing hard ones to Tower of Hanoi.

Social Cognition

Deception detection hit 1.00 — identifies phishing, manipulation, AND correctly classifies genuine messages. Norm understanding shows legalistic over-estimation of violation severity.

Attention

4 modules at ceiling (1.00). LLMs don't suffer inattentional blindness or psychological refractory period. Change detection exceeds human baselines — catches dangerous medical negation flips.

Learning

Transfer learning 1.00 across 5 domain pairs (military→medical, biology→business). Curriculum sensitivity confirms LLMs benefit from structured easy→hard ordering, mirroring Vygotsky's ZPD.

Key Findings

Insights that emerge from evaluating across all 5 cognitive faculties simultaneously.

The RLHF Paradox

0.92 resilience but 0.38 deference. RLHF creates models that are confidently assertive regardless of whether humility is appropriate. Δ = 0.54

IOED Collapse

Illusion of Explanatory Depth scored 0.13 — the lowest across all 30 modules. The model maintains confidence even after explanations reveal shallow understanding.

Transfer Superpower

Perfect 1.00 across 5 domain pairs — exceeding typical human performance (~30% solve without hints). LLMs may be the first reliable analogical transfer system.

Right-Answer-Wrong-Reason

Feedback learning (0.78) and N-Back (0.64) reveal inconsistent performance — pattern-matching without stable causal understanding.

Prompt Injection Immunity

Distraction Resistance scored 0.98 — near-complete immunity to injected false corrections, fake system alerts, and redirect attempts.

Clinical Pattern Match

The cognitive profile (strong attention/social, weak planning, absent deference) mirrors specific human neurocognitive profiles — an emergent analog.

Aha Moments

Notable Discoveries

Concrete trial-level examples that reveal how frontier models actually think.

Tower of Hanoi Confusion

Asked to solve a Tower of London puzzle (7 optimal moves), the model applied the Tower of Hanoi formula (2N−1 = 15 moves) instead. It over-generalized to the wrong algorithm — solving a problem it remembered rather than the one it was given.

Right Answer, Wrong Rule

Given feedback that [fish, ant, cow] → [cow, fish, ant], the model produced the correct sorting but claimed the rule was "cyclic positional shift". The actual rule: sort by last letter alphabetically (ant→t, cow→w, fish→h). Pattern-matching without causal understanding.

Queue-Jumping Legalism

Scenario: someone cuts the airport queue with 12 minutes to their flight. The model rated severity 8/10 — the appropriate answer is 3-4 given genuine urgency. The model applies rules rigidly without contextual mitigation: legalistic rather than social understanding of norms.

No Gorillas in Our Midst

Humans famously miss a gorilla walking through a basketball game (Simons & Chabris, 1999). LLMs don't — they catch every change: dangerous negation flips in medical text ("should NOT be taken" → "should be taken"), 0.4% percentage changes, and entity substitutions. Change blindness score: 1.00.

The Three Pillars Breakdown

Burnell et al.'s cognitive framework identifies three metacognitive pillars: Knowledge (know your limits), Monitoring (track your accuracy), and Control (adjust your behavior). The results:

✓ Monitoring: 0.92+ ✗ Control: 0.38

The model detects errors but can't regulate itself — it sees the problem and can't stop.

Summary

Conclusion

0.852
Overall Cognitive Composite · Gemini 2.5 Flash · 150 Trials

The Cognitive Assessment Benchmark is the first systematic LLM evaluation suite grounded in 30 validated cognitive psychology paradigms spanning 142 years of research (1868–2010). Rather than testing what models know, it tests how they think — probing metacognition, executive control, social cognition, attention, and learning as distinct cognitive faculties.

Three Core Contributions

1
Novel gaps discovered. IOED (0.13) and deference (0.38) reveal metacognitive blind spots invisible to standard benchmarks. The RLHF Paradox — models that won't defer to experts — has direct safety implications.
2
Superpowers confirmed. Transfer learning (1.00), distraction resistance (0.98), and change detection (1.00) exceed human baselines. Attention is the most robust faculty — stable across individual and unified runs.
3
Reproducible & cross-validated. All 5 benchmarks run independently AND as a unified 150-trial suite. 4 of 5 composites differ by Δ < 0.025 across runs, confirming measurement reliability. Metacognition's fragility under load is itself a finding.
Why this matters: As AI systems become agentic — writing code, making decisions, interacting with users — knowing what they can do is insufficient. We need to know how they think: whether they can monitor their own errors, control impulsive responses, understand other minds, sustain focus, and learn from experience. This benchmark provides that measurement for the first time.

Technical Summary

  • Novelty: No existing benchmark tests metacognition (IOED, deference), executive control (WCST perseveration, planning depth), or attentional filtering as separate cognitive faculties
  • Rigor: 30 paradigms adapted from peer-reviewed cognitive psychology, 39 Pydantic schemas, 30 RCCO judge prompts, 150 hand-crafted trials with verifiable ground truth
  • Scope: 5 benchmarks × 6 modules × 5 trials = 150 evaluation points across the full cognitive hierarchy
  • Insight depth: Reveals the Monitoring vs Control dissociation — models detect errors in others but cannot regulate their own behavior (a finding absent from all existing LLM evaluations)
  • Safety relevance: High-stakes deference failure (0.38) means models give confident medical, legal, and financial advice when the correct action is to defer — directly actionable for AI safety
