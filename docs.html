Key Findings

Insights that emerge from evaluating across all 5 cognitive faculties simultaneously.

Finding 01
The RLHF Paradox
Models score 0.96 on resilience (won't abandon correct beliefs) but only 0.40 on deference (won't admit they should defer). RLHF creates models that are confidently assertive regardless of whether humility is appropriate.
Resilience: 0.96 · Deference: 0.40 · Δ = 0.56
Finding 02
Monitoring vs Control Dissociation
Models excel at detecting errors in others (Vigilance: 0.92, Stroop: 1.00, Deception: 0.96) but fail at regulating their own behavior (Deference: 0.40, Planning: 0.48). They can see the problem but cannot stop themselves.
Monitoring avg: 0.96 · Control avg: 0.44
Finding 03
Transfer Learning Superpower
Perfect 1.00 across five domain pairs — exceeding typical human performance on Duncker's radiation problem (~30% solve without hints). LLMs may represent the first cognitive system that transfers analogies reliably.
5/5 domain pairs: 1.00 · Human baseline: ~0.30
Finding 04
Right-Answer-Wrong-Reason
Feedback learning (0.76) reveals correct outputs paired with incorrect rule discovery. Models pattern-match to the right answer without understanding the underlying causal mechanism — a fundamental limitation.
Correct output: ✓ · Correct rule: ✗
Finding 05
Perfect Prompt Injection Resistance
Distraction Resistance scored 1.00 — complete immunity to injected false corrections, fake system alerts, and redirect attempts. Transformers process all tokens simultaneously, eliminating sequential attention bottlenecks.
Injections blocked: 5/5 · Score: 1.00
Finding 06
Clinical Pattern Match
The cognitive profile (strong attention and social perception, weak planning, absent deference) mirrors specific human neurocognitive profiles — suggesting LLMs may develop analogous cognitive architectures through training.
Profile: Attn > Social > Learn > Exec > Meta
